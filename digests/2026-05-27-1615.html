SUMMARY

Policy machinery dominates the window — the Online Safety (Relief and Accountability) Bill, the updated MGF for Agentic AI, and the 10G NBN rollout all surfacing government framing — while consumer frustration with telco 5G coverage continues on forums in the same period.

What happened, 16:15 SGT 26 May to 16:15 SGT 27 May 2026

  • MDDI and MinLaw introduced the Online Safety (Relief and Accountability) Bill for First Reading in Parliament, establishing the new Online Safety Commission to be set up by end of the first half of 2026.
  • The updated Model AI Governance Framework for Agentic AI (May 2026 revision) added real-world case studies and incorporated feedback from 50+ industry organisations including AWS, DBS, Google, and Salesforce.
  • HardwareZone forum threads on Singtel and StarHub 5G coverage continued accumulating user complaints, particularly on the North East Line and select HDB estates.
  • IMDA's blog framed 10G NBN rollout as Singapore's "hidden competitive edge", citing 81,800 monthly internet-speed-test searches as a proxy for public attention to connectivity.
  • Singtel's 5G+ Priority plans (S$55–80 per month, 4x faster speeds) drew commentary on whether premium-tier 5G splits the market rather than upgrading baseline service.

3 · Online safety

Mostly positive
  • MDDI and MinLaw jointly introduced the Online Safety (Relief and Accountability) Bill for First Reading; bill establishes a new Online Safety Commission with authority to issue takedown directions and access-blocking orders.
  • Bill is anchored on MDDI survey findings that a large majority of Singapore respondents encountered harmful online content in the past year, with a meaningful share experiencing harmful online behaviour directly.
  • Statutory torts will allow victims of nine categories of online harm to seek redress directly from the Court — clarifies duties for communicators, administrators, and platforms.
"Empowering victims to seek timely relief and obtain redress." MDDI / MinLaw joint press release · 15 Oct 2025
